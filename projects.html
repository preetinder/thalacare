Our Projects

From mobile screening units to patient support networks and policy advocacy — a full picture of our work.

Active projects

Pre-marital Screening Drive — Maharashtra 2025

Our most ambitious project to date. We are partnering with 80+ hospitals, diagnostic centres, and marriage registration offices across Pune, Nashik, Solapur, Kolhapur, and Aurangabad districts to offer free HPLC tests to every couple registering for marriage. Goal: 50,000 couples screened by December 2025. Progress so far: 18,400 couples screened (as of June 2025).

Prevention Ongoing Maharashtra-wide
Active

School Awareness Programme — Pune District

Educational sessions on genetics, blood disorders, and pre-marital testing for students in Grades 9–12, delivered across 120 schools in the Pune Municipal Corporation and Pune district. Our team of trained educators uses visual models, Q&A sessions, and multilingual materials (Marathi, Hindi, English) to make the content accessible. Programme duration: April 2025 – March 2026. Students reached so far: 22,000.

Patient Support Network — Phase 2

Building on Phase 1, we are expanding our peer support and counselling network for Thalassemia Major patients and their families. Phase 2 adds: monthly online peer group sessions, a dedicated WhatsApp helpline staffed by trained counsellors (Mon–Sat, 9 AM–6 PM), and a financial aid referral directory connecting families with government schemes and CSR funds.

What We've Done

Completed projects

Patient Support Network — Phase 1 (2023–24)

Established a peer support and counselling network for Thalassemia Major patients and their families across Pune and Nashik. Delivered in-person monthly support meetings, trained 12 peer counsellors, and connected 1,200+ families to healthcare resources and financial aid. Phase 2 now underway.

Completed

Mobile Screening Units — Rural Maharashtra (2022)

Deployed 5 mobile HPLC testing units covering 230 villages across Nashik, Kolhapur, and Aurangabad divisions over 8 months. Total screened: 18,400. Carriers identified and counselled: 620. The mobile units operated in partnership with Zilla Parishad health teams.

Completed

Government Partnership Initiative (2021)

Advocacy and engagement project that successfully secured Maharashtra Government recognition of ThalaCare India as an official NGO partner for state Thalassemia prevention efforts. This unlocked access to government health infrastructure for all subsequent camps.

PMC School Programme — Pilot (2019–20)

Pilot genetics and awareness programme in 22 Pune Municipal Corporation schools. Reached 8,500 students in Grades 9–12. Results from the pilot informed the design of our current large-scale School Awareness Programme running across 120 schools.
