About ThalaCare India

We are a non-profit organisation working to eliminate new cases of Thalassemia Major in India — one test, one couple, one family at a time.

Who We Are

Born from a need to act

ThalaCare India was founded in 2015 by a group of haematologists, genetic counsellors, and Thalassemia-affected families who came together with a single shared belief: no child should be born into a life of monthly blood transfusions when a simple pre-marital test could prevent it entirely.

Since our founding, we have screened over 85,000 individuals, counselled thousands of at-risk couples, and partnered with hospitals, government bodies, and community organisations to build a prevention-first approach to Thalassemia across Maharashtra and beyond.

We are registered under Section 12A of the Income Tax Act. All our screening camps, awareness programmes, and counselling services are provided completely free of charge to the communities we serve.

Our Story

How we got here

From a small awareness meeting in Pune to a state-wide network — our journey over a decade.

2015

Founded in Pune

ThalaCare India is established by Dr. Meera Joshi and a group of eight haematologists and patient advocates. Our first awareness camp is held at Tilak Smarak Mandir with 280 attendees.

2017

First Mobile Screening Unit

We launch our first mobile HPLC testing unit, enabling us to reach villages with no access to diagnostic labs. The unit covers 60 villages in Nashik district in its first year.

2019

School Programme Launch

Partnership signed with Pune Municipal Corporation to deliver genetics and awareness education in all PMC schools. The programme reaches 18,000 students in its first year.

2021

State Recognition

Maharashtra Government officially recognises ThalaCare India as a preferred NGO partner for Thalassemia prevention, enabling access to government health infrastructure for our camps.

2023

Patient Support Network

We expand beyond prevention into patient support — connecting Thalassemia Major patients and their families with peer support groups, financial assistance, and quality-of-life resources.

2025

Pre-marital Screening Drive

Ambitious new programme: partnering with 80+ hospitals and marriage registration offices to offer free HPLC tests to every couple registering for marriage in Pune and Nashik districts.
