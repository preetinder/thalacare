Understanding the Condition

What is Thalassemia?

Thalassemia is a genetic blood disorder where the body makes abnormal or insufficient haemoglobin. People can carry the gene without any symptoms — but two carriers having a child face serious risks. A simple pre-marital blood test reveals your status in minutes.

●

Thalassemia Minor

One abnormal gene inherited. The person lives a healthy, normal life but can pass the gene to children. No treatment needed — but knowing matters enormously before marriage.

●

Thalassemia Major

Both parents pass the defective gene. The child requires blood transfusions every 2–4 weeks for life. Severe anaemia, organ damage, and a significantly reduced quality of life.

●

Thalassemia Intermedia

A moderate form between minor and major. May need occasional transfusions. Symptoms vary widely depending on the specific gene mutation inherited from each parent.

Pre-marital Awareness

What does it mean for your child?

If both partners are carriers, there is a 25% chance with every pregnancy that the child will have Thalassemia Major. Understanding your risk before marriage empowers you to make informed decisions.
